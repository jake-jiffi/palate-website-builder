# Website style taxonomy

Six archetypes that cover the spectrum of Jiffi client work. Every reference site and every client project is classified into one primary style (occasionally a secondary).

## 1. Product / SaaS
Software-first, feature-heavy, conversion-focused. The hero shows the product itself, often in motion. Strong feature grids, integration logos, pricing tables. Optimised for trial signup.
Reference exemplars: Linear, Stripe, Vercel, Cursor, Posthog.

## 2. Service / Consultancy
Sells expertise rather than a product. Case-study driven, proof-of-past-work, team-forward. The hero positions the offer and the people behind it.
Reference exemplars: Anthropic, Basement Studio, Resend.

## 3. Story / Editorial
Narrative-led, brand-heavy, long-form. The hero is an idea, not a product. Generous typography, considered imagery, reading-focused layouts.
Reference exemplars: Apple Newsroom, Stripe Press, Mercury blog.

## 4. Operational / B2B utility
Utility-first, information-dense, trust-driven. The hero explains the system. Documentation-adjacent, technical credibility, dense but legible.
Reference exemplars: Plain, Liveblocks, Tinybird.

## 5. Consumer / Lifestyle
Emotion-led, aesthetic-first, frequently photography-heavy. The hero is a feeling. Editorial imagery, restrained type, mood over information.
Reference exemplars: Aesop, Patagonia, Gelato Messina.

## 6. Portfolio / Creative
Work-led, often experimental, the designer or studio is the product. Bold motion, unconventional layouts, high craft.
Reference exemplars: Active Theory, Locomotive, The Brand Identity.

## How to classify a client brief

Read the brief for: what they sell (product vs expertise vs feeling), who buys it (technical vs business vs consumer), and how they win (features vs proof vs aesthetics vs craft). Map to the closest archetype. When a brief straddles two (common for Product + Service), pick the primary by what the homepage hero must do first.
