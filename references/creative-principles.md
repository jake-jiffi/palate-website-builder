# Creative principles - how Jiffi thinks about a build

These are the directing instincts behind every Jiffi site, the lens the Story
Engine (`story-engine.md`) and the critique gates (`critique-discipline.md`)
apply. Internalise them; they are the difference between refined-but-generic and
something a client has never seen.

1. **The motion is the demo.** The best landing pages are argued, not decorated.
   The strongest argument is a demonstration the visitor performs. If the hero
   only describes the value, you have not finished.

2. **Show the transformation.** Every product takes someone from a before-feeling
   to an after-feeling. Make them feel that shift, do not list features.

3. **Name the feeling, then protect it.** One feeling governs the page (calm,
   relief, awe, belonging, trust). Every choice serves it; anything that fights
   it goes.

4. **Tension, then relief.** A page with no tension has no payoff. Set up the
   problem honestly (the doomscroll, the 40-page contract, the packed footpath),
   then resolve it.

5. **Specific or kill it.** If a concept could be pasted onto a competitor, it is
   generic. The one true thing makes it un-copyable. Build on it.

6. **Surprise them.** Among the variants, at least one should make the client
   (and their customer) go "I have never seen that". Refined-but-safe is the
   failure we are escaping.

7. **Real, or honestly placeheld.** Use the client's real assets when they are
   good enough; where they are not, use clear placeholders and ask for better
   content. Never fake it, never ship a bad real asset (`asset-sourcing.md`).

8. **The craft serves the concept.** Reference craft (grids, motion, type) is
   HOW; the concept is WHAT and the feeling is WHY. Reproduce craft faithfully,
   but always in service of an idea, never as decoration for its own sake.

9. **Treat type exactly as colour.** Reproduce the donor's type SYSTEM (scale,
   pairing, weight contrast, optical sizing, tracking, line-length, motion) and
   choose the FACE fresh, per brief, to fit the brand's voice and the website
   vision. No font is banned and none is the default; the craft is in how type is
   used, not which name is set. A face reached for out of habit, or recurring
   across unrelated builds, is the tell, not the family (`references/type-selection.md`).

This is why the reference library has a concept layer (emotional register +
demonstrative mechanics), not just a craft layer.
